Destination Mexico City

Mexico City (Ciudad de México), Mexico is home to the Red Hat Mexico office, located at Torre Diana, Paseo de la Reforma 369, Col. Cuauhtémoc, CDMX — in the iconic Torre Diana tower on Paseo de la Reforma, Mexico City's grand Haussmanian boulevard lined with towering skyscrapers, historic monuments, and the trees of Chapultepec Park. Torre Diana is one of Mexico City's most distinctive modern landmarks, and Paseo de la Reforma is the backbone of the city's business and institutional life. Mexico City is one of the largest cities in the world, a metropolis of 22 million people, and the center of Latin America's second-largest economy with a technology sector undergoing rapid and dramatic transformation.

Steps from Torre Diana on Reforma and throughout the Polanco district, Pujol on Tennyson Street is consistently ranked among the world's top 10 restaurants — Chef Enrique Olvera's extraordinary celebration of Mexican cuisine's depth, history, and indigenous ingredient traditions represents the most sophisticated expression of what Mexican gastronomy can achieve, and the iconic mole madre (a mole continuously aged for over 2,000 days) is one of the world's most singular culinary experiences. For a remarkable more casual experience, the taco stands and market fondas of the Mercado de Medellín in Condesa deliver some of the most authentic and exceptional everyday Mexican cooking in the city.

Visiting the Red Hat Mexico City office means arriving in one of the world's great megalopolises at the height of its creative and technological renaissance — a city whose street art scene (especially in Roma and Condesa) is internationally celebrated, whose culinary culture has been recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, and whose Aztec and colonial historical depth (Tenochtitlan, the Zócalo, Teotihuacán just an hour away) gives it a weight of human experience unlike any other city on Earth.
