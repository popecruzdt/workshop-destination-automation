Destination Warsaw

Warsaw, Poland is home to the Red Hat Poland commercial office, located at ul. Domaniewska 37, 02-672 Warsaw — in the Mokotów business district, Warsaw's primary corporate and technology hub, home to the Polish offices of many of the world's largest technology companies. Warsaw is Poland's capital and largest city, a metropolis that has undergone one of the most remarkable urban transformations in European history — rebuilt from near-total wartime destruction to become a modern, vibrant, and economically dynamic city. Poland's growing enterprise software market and its deep engineering talent pool make Warsaw a strategic location for Red Hat's Central and Eastern European operations.

In the Mokotów district and across Warsaw, Senses Restaurant on Bielańska Street holds one Michelin star and is recognized as Warsaw's finest creative dining establishment — Chef Andrea Camastra's extraordinary tasting menu blends Italian technique with Polish ingredients in a progression of dishes that captures both the chef's heritage and his adopted city's remarkable creative energy. For a quintessentially Warsaw experience, Bar Mleczny (the iconic Polish milk bars) in the city center offer an authentic, affordable, and delicious window into traditional Polish daily life — the żurek sour rye soup and pierogi are genuine masterpieces of simplicity.

Visiting the Red Hat Warsaw office means arriving in a city whose history — from the Warsaw Uprising to the Solidarity movement to its post-1989 economic renaissance — gives it a depth of character and resilience that is genuinely inspiring. The Warsaw Old Town (completely reconstructed from historical records after the war) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the POLIN Museum of the History of Polish Jews is one of the world's most significant and beautifully designed historical museums, and the Palace of Culture and Science tower gives a panoramic perspective on just how much city has been built in the past 70 years. Warsaw's tech community is thriving, ambitious, and clearly in a hurry to make up for lost time.
