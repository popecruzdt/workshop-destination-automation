Destination Munich

Munich, Germany is home to the Dynatrace Germany office, located at Konrad-Zuse-Platz 8, Munich, 81829 — a fitting address named after Konrad Zuse, the engineer who built the world's first programmable computer. Munich is Bavaria's capital, Germany's most vibrant tech hub after Berlin, and the city where BMW, Siemens, and MAN were all born. The Dynatrace Munich office carries on this tradition of precision German engineering — now applied to intelligent cloud observability.

Near the Dynatrace office in the Neuperlach tech district, and throughout the greater Munich area, the dining scene is extraordinary. Tantris in Schwabing holds two Michelin stars and is widely considered one of Germany's great culinary institutions, with an impeccably curated menu that has been defining refined Bavarian and European cuisine for decades. For something quintessentially local, the Augustiner-Keller beer garden on Arnulfstrasse is one of Munich's most beloved — a sea of wooden benches under ancient chestnut trees serving the city's finest beer directly from wooden barrels, unchanged since 1812.

Visiting the Dynatrace Munich office means exploring a city where the Oktoberfest, the English Garden (larger than Central Park), and the Deutsches Museum — the world's largest science and technology museum — all coexist with world-class orchestras and one of Europe's most concentrated tech ecosystems. The Munich engineering community is serious, precise, and deeply proud of its work — very much the spirit that named their office after the father of computing. Prost to that!
