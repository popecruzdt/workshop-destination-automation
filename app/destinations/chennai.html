Destination Chennai

Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India is home to a Red Hat India office, located at Olympia Cyberspace, SIDCO Industrial Estate, Guindy, Chennai 600 032 — in the Guindy industrial and IT corridor, one of Chennai's established technology zones with excellent connectivity to the rest of the city. Chennai is the capital of Tamil Nadu and South India's most important technology and manufacturing city — the Detroit of India for its automotive industry and the home of a deeply skilled and prolific IT and engineering workforce. Red Hat's Chennai operations contribute meaningfully to the company's global engineering and services delivery capabilities.

In Chennai, Copper Chimney on Nungambakkam High Road is one of the city's most consistently praised and celebrated restaurants, earning top marks for its exceptional North Indian cuisine — outstanding tandoor preparations, rich Mughlai curries, and a warm, refined atmosphere that has made it a business dining institution in Chennai for decades. For an essential South Indian experience, Hotel Saravana Bhavan (a Chennai original that became one of the world's most widespread South Indian restaurant chains) offers the platonic ideal of masala dosa, idli sambar, and filter coffee that defines South Indian breakfast culture worldwide. The original on Usman Road is the authentic article.

Visiting the Red Hat Chennai office means coming to a city whose ancient Dravidian cultural identity — expressed in Carnatic music, Bharatanatyam dance, and some of India's most spectacular temple architecture at Kapaleeshwarar and Parthasarathy — coexists with a modern IT sector of global importance. Marina Beach, one of the world's longest urban beaches, stretches 12 kilometers along the Bay of Bengal and offers a sunrise walk that resets any engineering problem with remarkable efficiency. The Tamil Nadu people are known for their intellectual pride, their devotion to education, and their uncompromising standards of quality — all qualities that serve a Red Hat engineering team exceptionally well.
