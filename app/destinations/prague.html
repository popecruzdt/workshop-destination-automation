Destination Prague

Prague, Czech Republic is home to a major Red Hat engineering office, located at U Garáží 1611/1, Holešovice, 170 00 Praha 7 — in the Holešovice district, one of Prague's most vibrant and creatively charged neighborhoods on the north bank of the Vltava River, home to galleries, tech offices, and some of the city's finest restaurants and coffee shops. Prague is one of Red Hat's most important global engineering centers and the Czech Republic has produced exceptional open source engineering talent for decades — the Czech team's contributions to the Red Hat portfolio span RHEL, Ansible, OpenShift, and deep upstream Linux work that continues to shape the entire ecosystem.

In the Holešovice neighborhood surrounding the office, Eska restaurant earns consistent critical acclaim as one of Prague's finest dining destinations — housed in a beautifully converted early-20th-century industrial bakery, serving a creative menu of Czech and Central European cuisine rooted in fermented, foraged, and locally sourced ingredients with a sophistication that has placed it firmly on the European culinary map. The sourdough bread from Eska's bakery alone is worth the visit. For an outstanding broader Holešovice food experience, the Manifesto and Náplavka riverside markets showcase Prague's outstanding craft food scene.

Visiting the Red Hat Prague office means exploring one of the world's most spectacularly beautiful and best-preserved historic cities — the entire Old Town, Lesser Town, and Prague Castle district together form one of UNESCO's most celebrated heritage sites, a city that looks like it was designed as the backdrop of a fairy tale but turns out to be a thriving, sophisticated European capital with an extraordinary engineering community. The Holešovice office neighborhood also houses the DOX Centre for Contemporary Art and the Vltava river cycling paths, creating an extraordinary work environment where medieval spires, industrial Bohemian character, and deep Linux wizardry coexist in perfect harmony.
