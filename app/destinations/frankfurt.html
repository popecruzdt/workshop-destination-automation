Destination Frankfurt

Frankfurt am Main, Germany is home to the Red Hat Germany financial hub office, located at Franklinstrasse 61–63, 60486 Frankfurt am Main — in the Bockenheim district, a vibrant mixed-use neighborhood of the city's western side, well connected to Frankfurt's financial center and airport. Frankfurt is Europe's financial capital — home to the European Central Bank, the Deutsche Bundesbank, and the Frankfurt Stock Exchange — and the city through which more transatlantic flights pass than any other in Europe. It is also home to one of Europe's most critical internet exchange points (DE-CIX), making it literally the physical crossroads of the European internet. Red Hat's Frankfurt presence directly serves the financial services sector's cloud-native transformation needs.

In the Bockenheim area and across Frankfurt, Lafleur in the Palmengarten botanical garden earns two Michelin stars and is widely acclaimed as Frankfurt's finest restaurant — Chef Andreas Krolik's exceptional vegetable-focused fine dining menu within the extraordinary setting of the historic Palm Garden is a Frankfurt dining experience without parallel. For something more quintessentially Frankfurterisch, the Apple Wine taverns (Apfelwein-Wirtschaften) of Sachsenhausen — particularly Zum Wagner and Dauth-Schneider — are Frankfurt institutions serving the city's beloved sour apple wine with handkäse cheese and green sauce that are as much a cultural experience as a culinary one. Essential for any visitor.

Visiting the Red Hat Frankfurt office means arriving in the city that is simultaneously Germany's financial nerve center and one of its most underrated cultural destinations. The Städel Museum is one of Europe's great art collections, the Museum Embankment (Museumsufer) concentrates more world-class museums per kilometer than almost anywhere in Germany, and the reconstructed medieval Römerberg old town square is remarkably atmospheric for a city that was largely rebuilt after the war. Frankfurt's most important cultural attribute, however, is its function as the city that connects everything to everything — a quality that resonates deeply with what Red Hat builds.
