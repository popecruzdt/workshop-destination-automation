Destination Westford

Westford, Massachusetts, USA is home to one of Red Hat's most important engineering campuses, located at 314 Littleton Road, Westford, MA 01886 — in the Route 495 technology corridor northwest of Boston, one of New England's most established enterprise software zones. Westford is the kind of quietly productive tech town where legendary open source engineering happens without fanfare: much of Red Hat Enterprise Linux's kernel engineering and OpenShift platform development has deep roots here, carried out by teams who have been contributing to upstream Linux and open source projects for decades.

A short drive from the campus, the Westford Inn restaurant and the surrounding Chelmsford and Groton town centers offer excellent classic New England dining. For a standout meal, The Inn at Groton on Main Street in nearby Groton earns consistent praise for its refined New England cuisine and warm colonial inn atmosphere. For something more relaxed, the Nashoba Valley Winery in nearby Bolton offers an exceptional farm-to-table bistro experience — locally grown produce, estate wines from the adjacent vineyard, and a pastoral setting that makes the entire experience feel like a reward for a productive sprint.

Visiting the Red Hat Westford campus means being surrounded by some of the deepest Linux expertise on Earth — engineers who can trace their kernel patches back years and who debate systemd with a level of passion usually reserved for sporting events. The campus sits amid the beautiful farmland and forests of the Nashoba Valley, the Nashua River Rail Trail runs nearby for outstanding cycling and running, and the apple orchards of the region in autumn are one of New England's great seasonal experiences. This is where the open source sausage is made — and it is magnificent.
