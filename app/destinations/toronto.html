Destination Toronto

Toronto, Ontario, Canada is home to Red Hat Canada, located at 325 Front Street West, Suite 300, Toronto, ON M5V 2Y1 — in the heart of Toronto's Entertainment District, steps from the CN Tower, the Rogers Centre and the waterfront of Lake Ontario. Toronto is Canada's largest city and its financial and technological capital, a deeply multicultural metropolis of 3 million people (6 million in the greater region) that consistently ranks among the most livable and innovative cities in North America. Canada's strong open source community, its exceptional university talent pipeline, and its growing cloud ecosystem make Toronto a critical hub for Red Hat's Canadian operations.

Steps from the Front Street West office, Canoe Restaurant and Bar on the 54th floor of the TD Bank Tower earns consistent acclaim as one of Toronto's finest and most celebrated dining experiences — a spectacular room with panoramic views of the city's skyline, Lake Ontario, and on clear days, the Niagara region, serving a menu of modern Canadian cuisine that showcases exceptional regional ingredients from coast to coast. The Ontario lamb, PEI oysters, and Canadian whisky cocktail program are outstanding. For something more casual and equally essential, Toronto's St. Lawrence Market — a short walk from the office — is North America's finest public food market and the source of the legendary Montreal-style smoked meat sandwich.

Visiting the Red Hat Toronto office means arriving at one of the few places on Earth where you can walk from a 54th-floor Michelin-quality restaurant to a world-class public market to a hockey arena within 15 minutes on foot, while being surrounded by arguably the most ethnically and culturally diverse population of any city in the world. Toronto's tech scene — anchored by the University of Toronto, MaRS Discovery District, and the Vector Institute for AI — is producing some of the most consequential research in machine learning and cloud computing on the planet. Canada is polite, productive, and genuinely excellent at open source. Please and thank you.
