Destination Chicago

Chicago, Illinois, USA is home to the Red Hat Midwest office, located at 20 W. Kinzie Street, Suite 1710, Chicago, IL 60654 — in the heart of Chicago's River North neighborhood, one of the most dynamic and walkable business and dining districts in the city, steps from the Chicago River and minutes from the Loop. Chicago is America's great second city — an architectural wonder, the birthplace of jazz improv, the home of deep-dish pizza theology, and a Midwestern technology hub of considerable and growing importance. Red Hat's Chicago team serves one of the most enterprise-rich markets in the country.

Steps from the Kinzie Street office, Smyth in Fulton Market holds one Michelin star and earns extraordinary notices for its inventive, seasonally obsessed American tasting menu — Chef John Shields and Karen Urie Shields craft a progression of dishes that celebrate Midwestern ingredients with extraordinary creativity and technical mastery while maintaining a warmth and humor that felt distinctly at home in Chicago. For something more iconic, Chicago Cut Steakhouse on North Wacker Drive is consistently ranked the city's finest steakhouse with its prime aged beef aging in the front window display and spectacular river views from every table.

Visiting the Red Hat Chicago office means arriving in a city where the architecture alone justifies the trip — the Chicago Architecture Center river cruise is one of the world's great educational experiences, the Art Institute's Impressionist collection is a global treasure, and Millennium Park with The Bean is simultaneously a world-class public artwork and the city's communal living room. The deep-dish pizza debate (Giordano's vs. Lou Malnati's vs. Pequod's) is a civic institution that generates more heat than most Kubernetes cluster failures. Chicago's legendary work ethic and no-nonsense pragmatism align naturally with Red Hat's open source culture. City of big shoulders, big ideas, big deployments.
