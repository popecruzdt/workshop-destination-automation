Destination Austin

Austin, Texas, USA is home to the Red Hat Texas office, located at 7301 Ranch Road 2222, Building 1, Austin, TX 78730 — on the scenic western edge of Austin in the hill country corridor, surrounded by the cedar and limestone landscape of the Texas Hill Country. Austin has transformed over the past decade from a quirky college town into one of America's most important technology capitals — home to Tesla, Apple, Google, Meta, and Oracle campuses, and the host of South by Southwest (SXSW) which remains the world's most important intersection of technology, music, and culture. Red Hat's Austin office is a significant engineering and sales hub for the south-central US.

A short drive from the Ranch Road 2222 office into central Austin, Uchi on Lamar Boulevard is consistently ranked one of Austin's finest restaurants and nationally acclaimed for its extraordinary omakase Japanese cuisine with a creative Texas influence — Chef Tyson Cole's James Beard Award-winning cooking elevated Austin's culinary profile permanently when it opened and continues to set the standard. For something quintessentially Austin, Franklin Barbecue on East 11th Street is internationally recognized as one of the world's great barbecue experiences, a destination where pitmaster Aaron Franklin has applied artisan fervor and open source passion to the ancient art of Texas brisket. The line starts at dawn and is absolutely worth it.

Visiting the Red Hat Austin office means immersing yourself in a city that has officially and permanently kept itself weird, embraced it as a brand, and made it work beautifully in a technology context. The live music scene on 6th Street and Rainey Street is genuinely the finest in Texas, Barton Springs Pool in Zilker Park is the perfect close-of-day reset after a full day of OpenShift discussions, and the Texas Hill Country wineries just west of the city are a hidden gem that Austinites guard jealously but share generously with visitors. Keep Austin Weird — and cloud-native.
