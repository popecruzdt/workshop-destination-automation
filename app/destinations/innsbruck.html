Destination Innsbruck

Innsbruck, Tyrol, Austria is home to Dynatrace Lab Innsbruck, located at Höttinger Au 74, Innsbruck, Tyrol, 6020 — in the western residential and academic quarter of Austria's alpine capital. Innsbruck is famously ringed by the dramatic peaks of the Nordkette mountain range, which rise directly above the city to over 2,300 meters and are reachable by gondola in under 20 minutes from downtown. The Dynatrace Innsbruck team conducts serious engineering work with the world's best mountain view as motivation.

In central Innsbruck, Restaurant Stiftskeller is one of the city's most highly rated traditional Austrian restaurants, housed in a beautifully vaulted historic building and celebrated for its exceptional Tyrolean specialties — Tiroler Gröstl (pan-fried potatoes with eggs and beef), Wiener Schnitzel, and a warm, wood-paneled atmosphere that feels like the platonic ideal of Austrian hospitality. The Tyrolean dumplings and locally sourced alpine cheese board are essential ordering decisions.

Visiting the Dynatrace Innsbruck lab is a journey to one of the most spectacularly located offices in the entire Dynatrace network. The Nordkettenbahn gondola to the mountain top runs practically from the city center, ski runs begin at the edge of town, and in summer the Inn River cycling paths and alpine hiking trails make the work-life balance conversation here sound almost comically easy. The engineering is world-class; the scenery is genuinely unfair to everywhere else.
