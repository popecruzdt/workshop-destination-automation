Destination Singapore

Singapore is home to Dynatrace Asia-Pacific Pte Ltd, located at 250 North Bridge Road, #36-01/04, Raffles City Tower, Singapore, 179101 — on floors 36 of Raffles City Tower, one of Singapore's landmark mixed-use commercial towers in the heart of the CBD. Singapore is the most connected and digitalised country in Southeast Asia — a city-state that has systematically made itself the region's technology, financial, and logistics hub, and a government that has championed smart nation initiatives with exceptional consistency. As the regional headquarters for Dynatrace's Asia Pacific operations, Singapore is one of the most strategically important offices in the global network.

Steps from the Raffles City office, Odette at the National Gallery Singapore holds three Michelin stars and is consistently ranked one of Asia's best restaurants — Chef Julien Royer's luminous, seasonal French-European cuisine, impeccably executed and beautifully presented in the extraordinary colonial building of the former City Hall, represents Singapore fine dining at its absolute zenith. For something quintessentially Singaporean, the nearby Lau Pa Sat festival market and the Maxwell Food Centre in Chinatown are legendary hawker centres where Michelin-recognised chicken rice, laksa, and char kway teow are served at prices that make the value proposition quite literally world-class.

Visiting the Dynatrace Singapore office means arriving in a city that runs at the highest possible specification in almost every dimension — infrastructure, connectivity, safety, efficiency, and culinary excellence. Gardens by the Bay and its supertrees are a 10-minute walk, Sentosa Island is 20 minutes by cable car, and the hawker centre culture — where every Singaporean, regardless of walk of life, eats together at the same plastic tables over extraordinary food — is one of humanity's great democratic institutions. The Singapore team reflects the city's international, technically rigorous, and permanently forward-looking character in everything it builds and sells.
