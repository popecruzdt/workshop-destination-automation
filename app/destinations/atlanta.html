Destination Atlanta

Atlanta, Georgia, USA is home to the Red Hat Southeast office, located at 3 Ravinia Drive NW, Suite 1100, Atlanta, GA 30346 — in the Ravinia complex in Dunwoody/Sandy Springs, one of Atlanta's most important suburban commercial districts at the junction of I-285 and GA-400. Atlanta is the undisputed capital of the American South: home to the CDC, CNN, Coca-Cola, Delta Air Lines, and one of the fastest-growing tech sectors in the country. Atlanta's cloud and DevOps community is one of the most active in the Southeast, and Red Hat's presence here reflects the city's growing importance as an enterprise technology hub.

Near the Ravinia office and throughout Atlanta, Bones on Powers Ferry Road is consistently cited as Atlanta's finest steakhouse — a local institution since 1979 that has become the power-lunch and celebration destination of choice for Atlanta's business elite, with impeccably aged prime beef, exceptional service, and an atmosphere of classic Southern hospitality at its most polished. For a more contemporary experience, Lazy Betty in the Inman Park neighborhood holds a Michelin star and earns extraordinary reviews for its innovative American tasting menu rooted in Georgia ingredients and executed with exceptional technique.

Visiting the Red Hat Atlanta office means arriving in a city whose airport (Hartsfield-Jackson) is the busiest in the world — which means getting here from anywhere is straightforward and getting out is even easier. The BeltLine trail network winding through Atlanta's neighborhoods is one of urban planning's great recent achievements and a testament to what happens when a city decides to do something transformative. The Ponce City Market, the Fox Theatre, and the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site offer a city experience of remarkable cultural depth. Atlanta moves fast, thinks big, and the Red Hat team here is very much a reflection of the city's ambition.
