Destination Tysons

Tysons, Virginia, USA is home to the Red Hat Federal and Mid-Atlantic office, located at 8521 Leesburg Pike, Suite 700, Vienna, VA 22182 — in the Tysons Corner commercial corridor, one of the most strategically important business districts on the East Coast. Tysons sits at the heart of the Northern Virginia technology corridor — the region that hosts more data centers than any other geography on Earth and is home to AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google, and virtually every major cloud provider's US East operations. The Red Hat Tysons office serves both commercial enterprise clients and a significant portion of Red Hat's federal government technology portfolio.

Within the Tysons and Fairfax County area, Chez François in Great Falls earns consistent top regional recognition as one of Northern Virginia's finest dining destinations — a classic French country inn on the Potomac River that has been serving extraordinary French cuisine in an idyllic riverside setting since 1975. The Sunday brunch and seasonal prix-fixe menus are local legends. For a more contemporary experience, brb. (formerly Maple Ave Restaurant) in Vienna is a community-beloved restaurant earning top marks for its creative seasonal American cuisine and warm neighborhood character that offers a genuine contrast to the surrounding corporate environment.

Visiting the Red Hat Tysons office means planting yourself in the epicenter of America's cloud infrastructure geography — a location where the phrase "edge latency" is not just a talking point but a physical reality you're sitting on top of. Washington D.C. is a 20-minute Metro ride on the Silver Line from Tysons Corner, putting the Smithsonian museums, the National Mall, the Library of Congress, and the White House all within easy reach of a productive business trip. The convergence of federal technology procurement and commercial cloud innovation happening in this corridor is simply unlike anywhere else, and Red Hat is deeply woven into both.
