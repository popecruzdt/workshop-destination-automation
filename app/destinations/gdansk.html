Destination Gdansk

Gdansk, Poland is home to Dynatrace Lab Gdansk, located at Al. Grunwaldzka 411, Gdansk, 80-309 — in the dynamic Wrzeszcz district along Gdansk's main commercial artery, well connected to the city's historic center and the Tri-City metropolitan area including Gdynia and Sopot. Gdansk is a city of remarkable historical significance — birthplace of the Solidarity movement that changed the course of European history — and is today one of Poland's fastest-growing tech hubs. The Dynatrace Gdansk lab is one of the company's most important and prolific engineering centers.

In the heart of Gdansk's beautifully restored Long Market (Długi Targ), Restauracja Kubicki is one of the oldest and most celebrated restaurants in the city, consistently earning top marks for its exquisite traditional Polish and Kashubian cuisine — fresh Baltic fish, hunter's stew, amber-colored Baltic amber-infused vodka, and house-baked bread that sets the benchmark for the entire region. The waterfront location and historic interior make it one of the most atmospheric dining rooms in all of Poland.

Visiting the Dynatrace Gdansk lab means exploring a city whose amber-hued hanseatic architecture along the Motlawa River is one of the most photogenic urban waterfronts in Europe, whose beaches at Sopot are reachable by commuter rail in 20 minutes, and whose Europejskie Centrum Solidarności museum is one of the most powerful and moving historical experiences anywhere on the continent. The Gdansk engineering team is recognized across Dynatrace for its technical excellence, its collaborative spirit, and its proud Polish passion for getting things exactly right.
