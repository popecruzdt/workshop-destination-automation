Destination Tallinn

Tallinn, Estonia is home to Dynatrace Lab Tallinn, located at Sõle 14, Tallinn, 10611, Estonia — in the Põhja-Tallinn district, a rapidly evolving creative and tech hub in the Estonian capital. Tallinn is a city that has become synonymous with digital innovation: the birthplace of Skype, the country that pioneered e-residency and digital government, and consistently ranked one of the world's most advanced digital societies. The Dynatrace Tallinn lab is a natural fit for a city that takes software engineering as seriously as its medieval heritage.

In central Tallinn, Leib Resto ja Aed is one of the city's most praised restaurants, earning consistent top marks for its exquisite Estonian farm-to-table cuisine served in a beautifully restored historic townhouse in the Old Town. The menu celebrates Estonian ingredients — local black bread, fermented dairy, foraged mushrooms, Baltic fish, and seasonal produce — with a creativity and refinement that reflects a country quietly making itself a culinary destination. The garden patio in summer is one of Tallinn's most coveted dining spots.

Visiting the Dynatrace Tallinn lab means arriving in one of Europe's best-preserved medieval Old Towns — a UNESCO World Heritage Site of cobblestone streets, limestone towers, and guild halls that look exactly as they did in the 15th century, yet sit within walking distance of a country with digital infrastructure more advanced than most of Western Europe. The combination of genuine historical soul and technological fearlessness makes Tallinn one of the most interesting cities in the Dynatrace network to visit. Also: Estonian saunas are phenomenal and you should absolutely experience one.
